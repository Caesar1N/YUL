The Market
Gap

Three converging forces create a $1B opportunity in India's premium water segment.

I
The Plastic Crisis

1 million plastic bottles are purchased every minute globally. Consumers and regulators are demanding alternatives but the premium segment still lacks a credible, scalable replacement.

II
The Sustainability Premium

Luxury buyers are shifting spend toward brands with genuine environmental credentials. Greenwashing no longer works verifiable origin and packaging innovation command the premium.

III
The Source Transparency Gap

India's ₹1B+ premium water market is dominated by brands with opaque supply chains. Discerning consumers now demand traceable provenance and bio-active mineral quality.

The Bhutan
Advantage

The world's only carbon-negative country. A sovereign commitment to environmental stewardship that no competitor can replicate.

Environmental

Constitutional Mandate

72% forest coverage mandated by Bhutan's constitution since 1971. The source ecosystem is protected by sovereign law not a corporate CSR promise, but a national commitment.

Source

Legally Shielded Catchment

The spring's watershed is within a protected catchment zone. Industrial contamination, agricultural runoff, and development are constitutionally prohibited upstream.

Purity

Carbon-Negative Origin

Sourced from the same prestigious Himalayan spring as international award-winner Veen. The carbon-negative provenance is an unimpeachable brand credential no competitor can match.

Rights

Exclusive Access

Exclusive production and export rights secured through the Mash Foundation partnership. A structural moat that prevents replication by incumbent or emerging competitors.
